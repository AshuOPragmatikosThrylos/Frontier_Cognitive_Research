# Question Ecology

Questions do not exist in isolation. They share a limited environment and compete for resources.

## Competition For

### Compute

Processing power devoted to reasoning, hypothesis testing, and world model revision. Only some questions can be actively worked on at once.

### Memory

Storage for observations, lineage, state, and partial answers. Questions that persist consume memory whether or not they are currently investigated.

### Time

Attention over time. Long-lived questions compete with new ones for revisitation and sustained pursuit.

### Investigation

Active exploration — gathering data, running experiments, seeking observations that resolve or refine the question.

## Attention

Attention emerges from question competition. The system does not assign attention by fiat; it arises from which questions best justify the cost of compute, memory, time, and investigation under scarcity.
