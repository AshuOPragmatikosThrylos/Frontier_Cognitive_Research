# Order vs Chaos

Curiosity emerges between order and chaos — not at either extreme.

## Too Much Order

When the world model explains everything too easily, nothing surprises. Boredom. No compression failures, no new questions, no curiosity debt.

## Too Much Chaos

When nothing can be predicted or compressed, there is no stable expectation to violate. Confusion. Contradiction is everywhere but unstructured; questions cannot gain traction.

## The Middle

Curiosity emerges between order and chaos. Enough structure to form expectations; enough surprise to break them. Enough stability to investigate; enough tension to care.

Intelligence may thrive in this band — where questions arise, compete, and evolve without the system collapsing into noise or stagnation.
