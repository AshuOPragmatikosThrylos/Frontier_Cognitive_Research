# Question Reproduction

Questions produce child questions. Reproduction extends lineages and deepens inquiry without requiring the parent to die.

## Child Questions

Children arise through investigation. As a question is pursued, partial answers and new compression failures spawn narrower or related descendants.

**Example:** Investigating "Why don't penguins fly?" produces:

- Why did penguins lose the ability to fly?
- How do penguins survive without flight?

## Specialization

Questions reproduce through specialization. A parent question gives rise to children that focus on a subset of the original unknown.

Specialization is the primary mode of question reproduction — each child carries forward context while pursuing a more specific line of inquiry.

## Lineage

Reproduction builds genealogies over time. Parents may remain open while children advance understanding in parallel branches.
