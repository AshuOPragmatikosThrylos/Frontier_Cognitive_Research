# Question Immortality

Questions often outlive answers. An answer may be born, dominate, and die while the question that motivated it persists.

## Longer Lifetimes

Questions may possess much longer lifetimes than answers. The same question can survive many generations of provisional answers — each accepted, challenged, and replaced.

**Example:** "What is matter?" has outlived elemental, atomic, and subatomic answers. The question endures; the answers evolve.

## Persistence

Some questions persist for millennia. They go dormant, revive, split, and specialize — but they are not closed by any single answer.

Immortal questions are not necessarily always active. They may wait in dormancy until new anomalies or civilizations return attention to them.

## Contrast With Answers

Answers approximate. Questions endure. Intelligence may be measured less by which answers are held than by which questions remain alive across time.
