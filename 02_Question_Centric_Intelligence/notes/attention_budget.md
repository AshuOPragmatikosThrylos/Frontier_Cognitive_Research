# Attention Budget

## Definition

The finite pool of resources available for question investigation — compute, memory, time, and active exploration.

Not every question can be pursued at once. The attention budget forces prioritization.

## Implications

Only some questions receive investigation in a given period. Others wait, compete, or go without attention entirely.

## Question Starvation

A question that consistently loses competition for the attention budget receives little or no investigation. It may accumulate curiosity debt while remaining unresolved.

Starvation does not mean death. A starved question may still retain identity and history.

## Dormant States

Questions that lose access to the attention budget for extended periods may become dormant. They are set aside without resolution.

Dormant questions can revive when curiosity debt rises, related anomalies recur, or competition shifts in their favor.
