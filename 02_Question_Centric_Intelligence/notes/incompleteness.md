# Incompleteness

Perfect understanding implies no questions. If everything is known and compressed, nothing remains to ask.

## Chain

Without questions there is no curiosity.

Without curiosity there is no drive to explore, revise, or investigate.

Without curiosity there is no intelligence — at least not in the question-centric sense pursued here.

## Requirement

Intelligence may require incompleteness. A system that believes it has closed all unknowns stops generating questions, curiosity debt, and attention-worthy tension.

Enduring questions — civilizational, dormant, or meta — may be a feature of intelligence, not a failure of it.
