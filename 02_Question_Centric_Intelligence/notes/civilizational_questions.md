# Civilizational Questions

Civilizations inherit unresolved questions from previous civilizations. Questions cross epochs; answers do not always survive the transition.

## Inheritance

Each generation receives:

- Enduring questions still open or dormant
- Partial answers that may be challenged or obsolete
- Lineages and communities shaped by prior inquiry

Scientific and cultural progress is layered on inherited uncertainty, not a blank slate.

## Examples

### What is matter?

Asked across eras — from elements to atoms to fields and quanta. Answers have turned over; the question persists.

### What is life?

From vital force to metabolism to information and self-replication. Definitions evolve; the question remains.

### What is consciousness?

From soul to mind to brain to machine intelligence. Competing answers rise and fall; the question endures.

## Role

Civilizational questions anchor long-lived lineages and communities. They connect present inquiry to ancestral compression failures and motivate answers that will themselves be mortal.
