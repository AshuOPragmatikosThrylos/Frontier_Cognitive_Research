# Question Genealogy

Questions can form genealogies similar to biological evolution. Lineage tracks how understanding branches, merges, and deepens over time.

## parent_questions

Questions from which this question was derived. A child inherits context from its parents — the compression failures, observations, and curiosity debt that led to its birth.

**Example:** "Why did penguins lose the ability to fly?" has parent question "Why don't penguins fly?"

## child_questions

Questions spawned from this one during investigation. Children pursue narrower or related lines of inquiry while the parent may remain open.

**Example:** "Why don't penguins fly?" has children:

- Why did penguins lose the ability to fly?
- How do penguins survive without flight?

## generation

Distance from the original question in the genealogy. Generation 0 is a root question born directly from a compression failure. Each spawn or split increases generation by one.

**Example:**

- Generation 0: Why don't penguins fly?
- Generation 1: Why did penguins lose the ability to fly?
- Generation 2: What selective pressures favored swimming over flight in early penguins?

## ancestor_questions

All questions in the lineage above this one — parents, grandparents, and so on back to the root. Ancestors provide historical context for why a question exists.

**Example:** Ancestors of "What selective pressures favored swimming over flight in early penguins?" trace back through flightlessness and the original penguin observation.

## Genealogies

Like biological lineages, question genealogies branch, merge, and go extinct. Fit questions — those that generate useful understanding or sustained curiosity — are more likely to survive, spawn children, or merge into lasting abstractions.
