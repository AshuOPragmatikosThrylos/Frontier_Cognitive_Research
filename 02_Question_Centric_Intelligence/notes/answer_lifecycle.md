# Answer Lifecycle

Answers are temporary approximations. They serve the world model until better ones arrive or anomalies challenge them.

## States

### NEW

A proposed answer has just been formed. Not yet tested or integrated into the world model.

### ACCEPTED

The answer is incorporated into the world model. It compresses observations satisfactorily for now.

### DOMINANT

The answer is the prevailing explanation within its domain. It receives default use and resists replacement until challenged.

### CHALLENGED

New observations or compression failures undermine the answer. Competing answers may emerge; curiosity debt rises on related questions.

### OBSOLETE

The answer no longer fits available evidence or has been superseded. It may linger in memory but is no longer trusted for prediction.

### DEAD

The answer is discarded. It no longer competes for attention or shapes the world model.

## Temporary Approximations

No answer is final. Each state reflects how well an approximation holds under current evidence. Progress is the turnover of answers, not their permanence.
