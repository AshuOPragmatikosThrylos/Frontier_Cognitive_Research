# Question Communities

Clusters of related questions emerge naturally as the graph grows. Questions that share topics, observations, or lineage group into communities.

## Emergence

Communities form when:

- Questions repeatedly link through supports, depends_on, or generalizes/specializes.
- Shared compression failures spawn related question lineages.
- Investigation in one area generates child questions and neighbors.

Communities are not predefined. They arise from graph structure and sustained attention.

## Examples

### Physics

Questions about motion, force, energy, and spacetime cluster together. Progress in one area propagates fitness across the community.

### Biology

Questions about adaptation, evolution, ecology, and organism function form interconnected clusters — e.g. flightlessness, selective pressure, and aquatic survival linking penguin-related questions.

### Artificial Intelligence

Questions about representation, learning, reasoning, and attention budget form their own community — including meta-questions about question-centric intelligence itself.

## Role

Communities organize investigation. Attention may flow to a cluster when anomalies recur within a domain. Intelligence may be characterized by the health and evolution of its question communities, not only by isolated facts.
