# Question Graph

Questions connect to one another. Intelligence may be graph evolution rather than fact accumulation — growth comes from how questions relate, compete, cooperate, and reshape the network over time.

## Relationships

### supports

One question strengthens or reinforces another. Resolving or investigating A may help answer B.

### contradicts

Two questions pull in incompatible directions. Progress on one may undermine or conflict with the other until the world model reconciles them.

### depends_on

One question cannot be meaningfully pursued until another is at least partially resolved. B depends on A.

### generalizes

A broader question that subsumes more specific ones. "Why do some birds not fly?" generalizes "Why don't penguins fly?"

### specializes

A narrower question derived from a broader one. "Why did penguins lose flight?" specializes "Why do some birds not fly?"

## Graph Evolution

The question graph changes as questions are born, linked, merged, split, dormant, or abandoned. Understanding grows not only from storing answers but from how the graph of unresolved questions evolves.
