# Question Fitness

Fitness determines how likely a question is to win competition for the attention budget and to survive over time.

## Factors

### curiosity_debt

Accumulated pressure from unresolved anomalies. Higher debt increases the case for investigation.

### importance

Relative priority among questions. Some unknowns matter more for the world model or downstream decisions.

### novelty

Significance of the triggering anomaly. Surprising observations may boost fitness at birth and on recurrence.

### descendant_count

Number of child questions spawned. A productive parent may warrant continued attention to support its lineage.

### urgency

Time sensitivity. Some questions demand attention before observations decay or opportunities pass.

### recent_activity

How recently the question or its relatives were revisited or triggered. Stale questions may lose ground to active ones.

### historical_significance

Past impact on the world model or prior investigations. Questions that repeatedly led to insight may retain fitness even when dormant.

## Survival

Fit questions are more likely to receive attention, spawn descendants, merge into abstractions, or persist in dormant form until they can revive. Unfit questions are more likely to be abandoned or die.
