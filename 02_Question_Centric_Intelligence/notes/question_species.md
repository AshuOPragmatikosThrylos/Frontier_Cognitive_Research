# Question Species

Over time, related question lineages form stable families — question species that share domain, structure, and investigative tradition.

## Question Lineages

A lineage is a chain of parent and child questions tracing back to a root compression failure or deliberate formulation. Lineages branch through reproduction and occasionally diverge through mutation.

## Question Families

Families are clusters of lineages within a domain. They share vocabulary, methods, and graph connections. Families may merge, split, or go dormant as understanding evolves.

## Examples

### Physics Lineage

Root questions about motion and cause spawn descendants across mechanics, thermodynamics, and quantum theory. Shared depends_on and generalizes links bind the family.

- Why do objects fall?
- What is force?
- How does energy conserve across interactions?

### Consciousness Lineage

Questions about mind, experience, and cognition form a distinct family — often cross-linked to biology and artificial intelligence communities.

- What is consciousness?
- How does subjective experience arise?
- Can machine intelligence be question-centric?

## Emergence

Question species emerge over time. They are not designed upfront. They arise from sustained reproduction, mutation, and community formation within a domain.
