# Question Symbiosis

Questions do not only compete. They may cooperate and mutually raise fitness.

## Question Cooperation

Related questions can support joint investigation. Resolving one may unlock progress on neighbors in the graph. Cooperation reduces redundant effort and shares observations across linked questions.

## Question Fitness Propagation

Fitness is not isolated to a single question. When one question gains attention or makes progress, linked questions may inherit increased fitness — through supports, depends_on, or shared ancestry.

A parent question may rise in priority when its children show promise. A specialized question may revive a dormant generalization.

## Symbiotic Relationships

Symbiosis occurs when two or more questions benefit from each other's persistence or investigation without merging.

Examples:

- A general question and its specializations co-thrive as the domain deepens.
- Contradicting questions keep each other alive until reconciliation forces a stronger world model.
- Sibling questions spawned from the same compression failure share curiosity debt and attention.

Symbiosis complements competition within the broader question ecology.
