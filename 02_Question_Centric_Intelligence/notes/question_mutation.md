# Question Mutation

Question mutation is a change in how a question is framed, linked, or pursued — producing a variant that may diverge from its lineage.

## Novel Directions

Mutations may create novel directions. A reframed question, unexpected link, or cross-domain connection can open inquiry paths the parent did not imply.

**Example:** "Why don't penguins fly?" mutates into "What energy tradeoffs favor swimming over flight in aquatic birds?" — shifting from exception to optimization.

## Extinction

Failed mutations become extinct. Variants that do not accumulate curiosity debt, win attention, or produce useful descendants are abandoned or die.

Not every mutation survives. Selection acts on fitness as in broader question ecology.

## Creativity

Mutation is hypothesized to be a source of creativity. New questions need not arise only from compression failure; reframing and recombination may generate productive unknowns.

Mutation complements reproduction: reproduction narrows; mutation explores.
