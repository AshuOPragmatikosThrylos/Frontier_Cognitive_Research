# EXP-004 Failures

Post-experiment failure analysis for question communities.

Date: 2026-06-21  
Experiment: EXP-004 Question Communities

---

## Failure Analysis

No major failure was observed.

EXP-004 succeeded where EXP-003 failed. All nine questions remained ACTIVE. Three communities coexisted. Cross-community edges connected clusters without collapsing diversity. No attention collapse, no monoculture, no over-abstraction.

The experiment validates the hypothesis that graph communities are more ecologically stable than pure hierarchies for this anomaly set.

---

## Contrast with EXP-003 Failure

| Failure mode (EXP-003) | Status in EXP-004 |
|------------------------|-------------------|
| Over-abstraction | Not observed — no merge demotion |
| Attention collapse | Not observed — 9 ACTIVE questions |
| Diversity loss | Not observed — all levels preserved |
| Monoculture | Not observed — 3 communities |
| Excessive compression | Not observed — no staged absorption |

EXP-004 was designed explicitly to avoid EXP-003's failure mode. The absence of failure is an informative result, not a null outcome.

---

## Potential Future Risks

Although EXP-004 did not fail, the following risks remain for scaled or dynamic systems:

### Over-connected graphs

Adding too many cooperates_with edges could create a fully connected graph where all questions appear equally related. Attention allocation would become ambiguous — the opposite of hierarchy collapse, but similarly dysfunctional.

### Community collapse

If community merging were introduced without diversity safeguards, multiple communities could consolidate into one — reproducing EXP-003's failure in graph form.

### Loss of diversity

Without explicit ACTIVE preservation rules, future experiments adding merge or attention dynamics could demote entity questions despite graph structure.

### Monocultures

A single dominant community (e.g. Capability absorbing all attention) could starve Bird and Mammal clusters of investigation resources.

### Over-abstraction

Category and capability questions coexist with entities in EXP-004, but future auto-abstraction rules might still compress communities upward if merge logic is reintroduced.

---

## Limitations of EXP-004

Observations that constrain generalization:

1. **Communities were declarative**, not algorithmically discovered. Emergence was designed, not detected.

2. **Graph wiring was hardcoded.** supports, depends_on, and cooperates_with edges were manually specified, not inferred from observations.

3. **No attention dynamics.** All questions ACTIVE simultaneously; no competition or budget tested.

4. **Static snapshot.** No evolution, split, merge, or dormancy over time.

5. **Small graph.** Nine nodes and eleven edges — scalability untested.

These are not failures but boundaries on what EXP-004 demonstrates.

---

## Overall Assessment

Communities appear significantly more stable than pure hierarchies.

EXP-004 provides a positive baseline for graph-based question organization. The experiment supports H41, H42, H43, and the pivot away from unchecked hierarchical merging.

No corrective action required for EXP-004 itself. Future work should stress-test community stability under competition, cooperation, and dynamic reorganization — while preserving the diversity safeguards that made EXP-004 succeed.

---

## Conclusion

EXP-004 did not fail. It resolved the ecological failure mode identified in EXP-003 by replacing tree merge with typed graph communities. The primary research outcome is a viable alternative architecture — coexistence over convergence — warranting further investigation under dynamic conditions.

Potential failures remain hypothetical until tested at scale or under attention competition.
