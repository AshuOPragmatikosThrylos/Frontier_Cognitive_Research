# EXP-004 Results

Post-experiment summary for question communities.

Date: 2026-06-21  
Experiment: EXP-004 Question Communities

---

## Theme

Question Communities

Test whether questions form stable graph communities — rather than collapsing into a single hierarchical abstraction — while preserving diversity across abstraction levels.

---

## Experiment Summary

Questions formed communities rather than collapsing into a hierarchy.

EXP-004 responded to the over-abstraction failure observed in EXP-003. Instead of staged merges that demoted lower-level questions, the experiment built a graph of coexisting questions linked by typed relationships.

### Communities Observed

**Bird**

- q-penguin — Why doesn't Penguin fly?
- q-ostrich — Why doesn't Ostrich fly?
- q-emu — Why doesn't Emu fly?
- q-kiwi — Why doesn't Kiwi fly?
- q-bird-flightlessness — Why don't some birds fly?

**Mammal**

- q-bat — Why does Bat fly like a Bird?
- q-whale — Why does Whale swim like a Fish?
- q-mammal-capabilities — Why do some mammals gain unexpected capabilities?

**Capability**

- q-species-capabilities — Why do species gain or lose capabilities?

### Key Outcomes

- All questions remained **ACTIVE**
- Cross-community relationships emerged between Bird, Mammal, and Capability clusters
- No question was demoted to PARTIALLY_RESOLVED or DORMANT
- Graph structure replaced vertical merge hierarchy

## Supported Hypotheses

| Hypothesis | Evidence |
|------------|----------|
| **H18** — Question communities emerge naturally | Bird, Mammal, and Capability clusters formed with distinct membership |
| **H19** — Questions increase neighboring fitness | Entity questions support category questions; categories depend on capability bridge |
| **H39** — Intelligence requires diversity across abstraction levels | Entity, category, and capability questions all remained ACTIVE simultaneously |
| **H41** — Question communities are more stable than hierarchies | No attention collapse; contrast with EXP-003 (1 ACTIVE, 8 PARTIALLY_RESOLVED) |
| **H42** — Intelligence prefers graphs over trees | Typed edges replaced parent-child merge tree |

---

## Unexpected Observations

1. **Every question remained ACTIVE.** Unlike EXP-003, no state demotion occurred at any abstraction level.

2. **Hierarchies from EXP-003 did not collapse diversity.** The graph design explicitly avoided merge-based demotion.

3. **Cross-community edges appeared.** Five edges connected Bird, Mammal, and Capability communities — not only internal cluster links.

4. **Question communities coexisted.** Three communities persisted in parallel without one absorbing the others.

---

## Surprising Observations

1. **9 ACTIVE questions** — full diversity preserved across entity, category, and capability levels.

2. **11 edges** — supports (6), depends_on (2), cooperates_with (3).

3. **3 communities** — Bird (5 members), Mammal (3 members), Capability (1 member).

4. **5 cross-community edges** — capability question linked to both domain communities without absorbing them.

5. **Communities preserved diversity better than hierarchies.** Direct comparison with EXP-003: same underlying anomalies, opposite ecological outcome.

---

## New Hypotheses

| Hypothesis | Statement |
|------------|-----------|
| **H43** | Intelligence is sustained by coexistence rather than convergence |
| **H44** | Communities are the natural units of cognition |
| **H45** | Cross-community edges drive creativity |

H43–H45 arise from the contrast with EXP-003. Stability came from parallel coexistence, not upward convergence. The Capability community acts as a bridge rather than an apex predator of attention.

## Conclusion

EXP-004 demonstrates that question communities can preserve diversity where hierarchical merging failed. Graph structure with typed relationships allows entity, category, and capability questions to coexist as ACTIVE nodes. The experiment supports the pivot from trees to graphs proposed after EXP-003's over-abstraction failure.

Communities appear significantly more stable than pure hierarchies for this anomaly set.
